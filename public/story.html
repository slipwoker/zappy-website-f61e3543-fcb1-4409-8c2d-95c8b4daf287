Our Story

The Heart Behind the Project

There is a place in Israel where the water is quiet in the early morning, where the light touches the hills softly, and where faith once walked on the shore. It was on these very shores — beside the Sea of Galilee — that Rocks of Galilee was born.

Every time we visit this sacred place, we feel something that is difficult to put into words. A stillness. A nearness. A sense that the ground beneath your feet holds something eternal. And slowly, a question began to form in our hearts:

“ What about the people who long to visit this land but cannot? The grandmother who dreams of Israel but is too old to travel. The family who prays for the Holy Land every Sunday. The young believer who wants something real — something touched by this land. ”

We live here in Israel, not far from Bethlehem. And out of that question grew a simple, faithful answer. We began carefully gathering small stones from the shores of the Sea of Galilee — stones that have rested where miracles happened, that have touched the same waters Jesus crossed — and crafting them into handmade pieces designed to bring the Holy Land into your home.

These are not souvenirs. They are a bridge. A tangible, sacred connection to the land where Jesus walked, taught, called His disciples, calmed the storm, and walked upon the water. Rocks of Galilee exists so that every believer — wherever they are in the world — can hold a piece of that sacred shore.

The Story of the Sea of Galilee

There is a place in Israel where the water is quiet in the early morning, where the light touches the hills softly, and where faith once walked on the shore. The Sea of Galilee is not simply a lake. It is a living threshold between the ordinary and the eternal.

It is where Jesus walked beside fishermen on these very shores — men who mended nets, who knew the smell of fish and the weight of oars. It is where He called His first disciples, speaking words that changed the course of history: "Follow me, and I will make you fishers of men."

It is where He calmed the storm, standing in a battered boat while the water raged, and with a word brought stillness to the waves and wonder to the hearts of those who watched. Where He multiplied bread and fish on a hillside and fed thousands with what seemed like nothing at all.

"And He walked upon the water — and for one trembling moment, so did Peter."

Along the shores of this sea lie small stones. Simple. Quiet. Unnoticed by most. But these stones have rested where miracles happened. They have touched the same waters that Jesus crossed. They belong to the land where faith became history.

For Christians around the world, the Sea of Galilee is not simply geography — it is living Scripture. Every stone on its shore carries the memory of a morning when the world was changed forever. These are not merely rocks from Israel. They are fragments of the sacred ground where the story of salvation unfolded in human time.

This is why we gather them with care. This is why we bring them to you.
